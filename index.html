What I Design & Build

Protocol Architecture

I design and implement consensus-layer upgrades across multiple blockchain clients written in different languages — Go, JVM/Scala, and Java. This includes integrating execution-layer proposals spanning dynamic fee markets, account abstraction, and transient storage, then coordinating cross-client validation on live testnets. The core design challenge is ensuring behavioral equivalence across fundamentally different codebases while maintaining each client's architectural integrity.

Governance & Smart Contract Systems

I architect governance systems across the full complexity spectrum — from soulbound NFT-based voting to DAO frameworks to prediction-market futarchy using logarithmic scoring automated market makers. Alongside governance, I design name service registries, decentralized exchange infrastructure, and token launch platforms. Every system is backed by rigorous formal testing: fuzz testing, invariant checks, and security proofs, with individual contract systems exceeding a thousand tests.

Full-Stack Web3 Interfaces

The same architect who designs the smart contracts also designs and builds the TypeScript interfaces, on-chain analytics dashboards, and WebGL product experiences that sit on top. React, wagmi, viem, real-time RPC indexing, brand design systems — the full surface from chain interaction to user experience. No handoff between contract architecture and interface design means no translation loss between what the system does and how people interact with it.

Data Architecture

I design pipelines that process over a billion records across multiple large-scale datasets. This involves performance-engineering storage layers to achieve order-of-magnitude throughput improvements, building multi-pass enrichment architectures using LLM analysis, and constructing full-text search infrastructure that serves complex queries across millions of documents. The same systems thinking that governs protocol design also produces high-performance data systems.

How I Work

I treat development tooling as infrastructure, not afterthought. I've built AI-augmented development systems — custom agentic workflows that route tasks by complexity, automate mechanical operations at zero marginal cost, and enforce quality gates systematically. This is systems architecture applied to the development process itself: the same design discipline I bring to protocol work also governs how those systems get built.

The result is the ability to maintain genuine architectural depth across protocol engineering, smart contract design, frontend development, and data infrastructure simultaneously — not as a generalist skimming surfaces, but as an architect who has built the tooling to operate with rigor across a wider surface than is typically possible. I design systems that expand access to resilient financial infrastructure, and I ship what I design.
